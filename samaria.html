Intro / Challenge

Samaria is a social conferencing platform that bridges the gap between digital Christian missionaries and the audiences who need them. It's almost as if Clubhouse and Zoom had a child.

Samaria was born when an atheist came to know the true loving nature of The One true GOD through a virtual bible study group. After that experience, he wondered if it was possible to build a place where anyone, no matter their level of understanding in the Christian faith, can discover fellowships tailored to their spiritual needs. This eventually evolved into bold and exciting ideas regarding ways to connect the global Body of Christ with tools 21 Century tools.

  • Role: I designed the User Interface and also hired, managed and mentored a junior product designer
  • Tools: Figma + Pen & Paper.
  • Outcome: Design System, UI Design, & Interactive Prototypes.

Research & Insights

We spoke to Christians who practice their faith online. Two problems stand out:

  1. The main reason people join online fellowships is to solve specific needs. People who want to read the Bible more often join Bible study groups. Many even joined communities that cater to Christian entrepreneurs. The needs are diverse, and thousands of digital communities cater to them.

    Currently, the only way to find these fellowships is through an invitation or referral. There is no platform that lists digital missionaries and the experiences they provide. Due to the diversity of needs and experiences available in global Christianity; digital Christian fellowship needs its own discovery engine.

  2. Adopters of online fellowship also bemoan the lack of immersion. Physical interaction is a big part of Christian worship; this is lacking online. They also note that they more easily lose focus and stop paying attention to the fellowship. The chatbox is also an issue, as most conferencing platforms donâ€™t have an interactive chatbox. Zoom, Google Meet, and YouTube are the major suspects here. The Twitter spaces are better on this note, but the chat is just like tweeting. People want a digital experience that closely replicates physical fellowship.

Project Goals

We had two major user groups to design for. Organizations who seek a wider audience to reach & individuals who seek fellowships that align with their spirtual growth.

  1. Short-term goals: Build a simple discovery engine that helps Christians search, and participate in online fellowships that align with their spiritual goals.
  2. Long-term goal: We hope to create one of the first practical use cases for the Meta-verse, by empowering Christians to stream fellowships in Virtual Reality.Future releases will have interesting features like Event Hotspots, Interactive Chat, VR, Payments, and so on.
